### Spain

#### Spain Orders Probe Into X, Meta, and TikTok Over AI-Generated Child Abuse Material

Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez has directed prosecutors to investigate social media platforms X, Meta, and TikTok for allegedly enabling the spread of AI-generated child sexual abuse material. Sánchez cited data from Save the Children showing that one in five young Spaniards — mostly girls — say fake nude images of them were created by AI while they were minors. The move escalates Europe's growing clash with US tech giants over content moderation and child safety. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/spain-probe-x-meta-tiktok-over-ai-generated-child-sexual-abuse-material-2026-02-17/)

#### Five Young People Killed in Apartment Fire Near Barcelona

Five teenagers have died and at least five others were injured in a fire that broke out in an attic storage room of a five-story apartment building in Manlleu, a town of 21,000 about 80 km north of Barcelona. Firefighters said the victims were found burned beyond recognition. The cause of the blaze and why the victims could not escape remain under investigation. [BBC News](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c99jngpymyjo)

#### Storm Pedro: Spain's Tenth Named Storm of the Season Arrives

Spain is bracing for Storm Pedro, the tenth named storm of the year, bringing violent winds with hurricane-force gusts expected in Tarragona and heavy snow in the Pyrenees. The storm adds to what has been an exceptionally turbulent weather season following devastating flooding from storms Marta and Leonardo that displaced thousands. [The Olive Press](https://theolivepress.es/spain-news/2026/02/17/spain-tenth-storm-arrives)

#### Madrid-Seville High-Speed Rail Service Resumes After Deadly Adamuz Crash

High-speed train service between Madrid and Andalusia has resumed after weeks of suspension following the fatal derailment near Adamuz, Córdoba, which killed 46 people last month. Rail infrastructure manager Adif completed repairs to the damaged section, though some routes remain affected. The disaster was Spain's worst rail accident in decades. [Euro Weekly News](https://euroweeklynews.com/2026/02/16/back-on-track-madrid-sevilla-high-speed-trains-resume-after-adamuz-repairs)

#### Majority of Spaniards Want Constitutional Reform

A new survey from Spain's National Research Council (CIS) shows that 84.3% of Spaniards believe the Constitution needs reform, with 66% wanting "significant" changes. The 1978 Constitution, enacted after the end of Franco's dictatorship, has been a cornerstone of Spanish democracy but is increasingly seen as outdated by a large majority of the population. [The Local](https://www.thelocal.es/20260217/today-in-spain-a-roundup-of-the-latest-news-on-tuesday-67)

### Barcelona & Catalunya

#### Manlleu Apartment Fire: Catalunya in Mourning

The deadly fire in Manlleu that killed five teenagers has prompted an outpouring of grief across Catalunya. Regional authorities have opened an investigation into the blaze, which started in a storage room on the upper floor of the building on Monday evening. The Catalonia fire service is working to determine why the victims were unable to escape. [The Olive Press](https://theolivepress.es/spain-news/2026/02/17/five-teenagers-dead-after-fire-rips-through-apartment-building-near-barcelona)

#### Storm Pedro to Batter Tarragona with Hurricane-Force Winds

The newly formed Storm Pedro is expected to hit Catalunya hard, with the most severe impacts forecast for the Tarragona area, where hurricane-force wind gusts are expected. Snow is also forecast for the Pyrenees. Weather alerts are in effect across the region as authorities urge caution. [ElNacional.cat](https://www.elnacional.cat/es/tiempo-hoy/tiempo-catalunya-miercoles-18-febrero-nace-borrasca-pedro-ultimas-alertas-febrero-como-cuando-afectara-catalunya_1553546_102.html)

#### Catalonia Surpasses 200 International Technology Hubs

Catalonia now hosts over 200 international technology hubs, employing 46,080 workers, 38% of whom are foreign nationals. The milestone underscores the region's growing reputation as a European tech center, attracting major companies to establish research and development operations in Barcelona and surrounding areas. [Ara](https://en.ara.cat/economy/catalonia-now-has-over-200-international-technology-hubs_1_5651532.html)

#### Barcelona Awards 188M Euro Sewer Maintenance Contract

The city of Barcelona has awarded a 188 million euro contract to FCC for the maintenance of its sewer network. The new agreement places greater emphasis on structural inspections and periodic cleaning of storm drains to ensure rainwater follows its proper underground course — particularly relevant given the season's extreme weather events. [La Vanguardia](https://lavanguardia.com/local/barcelona/20260217/11468865/barcelona-adjudica-fcc-mantenimiento-alcantarillado-188-millones-euros.html)
