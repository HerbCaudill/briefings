### Spain

#### Police Sexual Assault Scandal Deepens as Interior Offers Protection to Whistleblower

Spain's Interior Ministry has assigned police protection to the female officer who filed a rape complaint against the former head of operations (DAO) of the National Police. New evidence including an audio recording and medical reports were presented to the court. The government commented that "no organization is free of sexist pigs," while senior police sources suggest Minister Marlaska may appoint a woman as the new DAO to address the crisis. #### CIS Poll Shows PP and PSOE in a Technical Tie Ahead of Castilla y Leon Elections

With just a week before the official campaign begins, Spain's public polling institute shows the conservative PP under Mañueco would win but fall short of an absolute majority, while PSOE closes the gap significantly. [RTVE](https://www.rtve.es/noticias/espana/), [La Vanguardia](https://www.lavanguardia.com/), [El Periodico](https://www.elperiodico.com/es/)

#### Rufian's Left-Unity Initiative Meets Widespread Rejection

Gabriel Rufian's proposal to unite Spain's fragmented left has been dismissed by virtually all potential allies — Podemos rejected it outright defending its autonomy, Sumar called for unity but on its own terms, and the broader left and independence movements remain focused on their own projects. ### Barcelona & Catalunya

#### Sagrada Familia Reaches Its Highest Point as Tower of Jesus Is Crowned at 172.5 Metres

A three-dimensional cross was placed atop the Tower of Jesus, 144 years after the first stone was laid at Gaudi's iconic basilica. The temple is now the tallest religious structure in Europe and nears completion of its most ambitious phase. [La Vanguardia](https://www.lavanguardia.com/), [El Periodico](https://www.elperiodico.com/es/), [3cat](https://www.3cat.cat/3catinfo/), [Tot Barcelona](https://www.totbarcelona.cat/)

#### Rodalies Commuter Trains Still Disrupted One Month After the Gelida Accident

Barcelona's commuter rail network continues to suffer delays and speed restrictions across 200 points a month after the derailment at Gelida. A new challenge looms with the upcoming closure of the Garraf tunnels for maintenance, threatening further chaos for commuters south of Barcelona. [El Periodico](https://www.elperiodico.com/es/), [El Periodico Barcelona](https://www.elperiodico.com/es/barcelona/), [3cat](https://www.3cat.cat/3catinfo/)

#### Illa Pledges "Total Commitment" to IRPF Tax Collection to Unlock Catalan Budget Deal with ERC

Catalan president Salvador Illa is working to secure ERC's support for the regional budget by reaffirming his promise to give Catalonia control over income tax collection — a key condition of the investiture pact. Business group Foment del Treball has called the budget agreement with the Comuns "philo-communist." [La Vanguardia](https://www.lavanguardia.com/), [El Periodico](https://www.elperiodico.com/es/), [3cat](https://www.3cat.cat/3catinfo/)

#### Barcelona Doctors Strike to Demand Better Working Conditions

Physicians took to the streets of Barcelona protesting for a separate collective agreement and improved working conditions, warning that the public health system risks collapsing. While official turnout was modest, the wave of appointment cancellations was described as unsustainable long-term. [El Periodico](https://www.elperiodico.com/es/), [3cat](https://www.3cat.cat/3catinfo/)

#### Pisarello Wins Barcelona en Comu Primaries to Be Mayoral Candidate

Gerardo Pisarello defeated writer and TV personality Bob Pop in the party primary and will be Barcelona en Comu's candidate for mayor in the next municipal elections, increasing pressure on Mayor Collboni from the left. [Tot Barcelona](https://www.totbarcelona.cat/), [3cat Barcelona](https://www.3cat.cat/3catinfo/barcelona-ciutat/)

#### Costa Brava: Storm Damage Cleanup Races to Meet Easter Deadline

Torroella de Montgri is urgently cleaning beaches battered by recent storms to have them ready for Semana Santa, while in Calonge the national coastal authority halted construction of new breakwaters due to technical errors. Meanwhile, 45% of the 26,000 homes in Palamos are not primary residences, highlighting the coast's ongoing housing pressure.
